Definition 1.6.1.

To prove that a statement \(P(n)\) is true for all integers \(n\ge 0\text{,}\) we use the principal of math induction. The process has two core steps:

  1. Basis step: Prove that \(P(0)\) is true.
  2. Inductive step: Assume that \(P(k)\) is true for some value of \(k \ge 0\) and show that \(P(k+1)\) is true.
