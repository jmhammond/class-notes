In each polynomial below, assume \(x\) is some variable. Use the binomial theorem to expand and reduce modulo the appropriate number:

  1. \((x+1)^2 \pmod 2 \)
  2. \((x+1)^5 \pmod 5 \)
  3. \((x+1)^7 \pmod 7 \)

Prove that \((x+1)^p \equiv x^p + 1 \pmod p\) for any prime \(p\text{.}\)
