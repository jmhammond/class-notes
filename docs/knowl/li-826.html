Item 3.a.

Since \(\gcd(3,26) = 1\text{,}\) there is a unique inverse. Using the Euclidean Algorithm we find that \(3^{-1} \equiv 9 \pmod{26}\text{.}\) Doing the algebra:

\begin{align*} 3x \amp= 19 \pmod{26}\\ (3^{-1})3x \amp=(3^{-1}) 19 \pmod{26}\\ x \amp\equiv (9) 19 \pmod{26}\\ x \amp\equiv 171 \pmod{26}\\ x \amp\equiv 15 \pmod{26} \end{align*}

is the unique solution.
