  2. Let \(g(x) = x^2\) be the mapping from \(\mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}\text{.}\)

  3. Let \(g(x) = x^2\) be the mapping from \(\mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z}\text{.}\)

  4. Let \(g(x) = x^3\) be the mapping from \(\mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z}\text{.}\)
